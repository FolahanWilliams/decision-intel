## 1. Core thesis

Decision Intel is **the native reasoning layer for every high-stakes call** — an AI-native audit + simulation engine that catches cognitive bias, predicts steering-committee questions, and produces a hashed, tamper-evident Decision Provenance Record on every strategic memo, board deck, or M&A diligence pack. The IP moat is the **Recognition-Rigor Framework** (R²F · Kahneman's debiasing arbitrated with Klein's recognition-primed decision in one 12-node LangGraph pipeline), the **Decision Quality Index** (DQI · platform calibration baseline Brier 0.258 across 143 audited corporate decisions), and the **19-framework cross-border regulatory map** (G7 + EU + GCC + African coverage that no US-only incumbent — Cloverpop, Aera, Quantellia, IBM watsonx.governance — can match).

**Decision system of record · positioning extension (locked v3.2).** Decision Intel is the *system of record for decisions specifically* — not slides, not customer interviews, not competitive analysis, not the company knowledge base. Just the decisions + their outcomes + the reasoning trail. The CSO value-prop extension that follows: *"All your deals and their outcomes live in Decision Intel. Decision history survives team transitions. Audit-committee Q&A pulls up reasoning in 60 seconds. Future decisions get sharper because the platform learns your specific bias patterns."* This is a value-prop extension, NOT a category change — never drift into "your full knowledge base." Stay narrow: decisions, outcomes, reasoning trail. The narrowness is the moat; the YC "request for startups · company knowledge base" framing is explicitly NOT what we are.

The cold-context bridge sentence (used on first-meeting / DM / conference 1:1 surfaces): *"We run 60-second audits on strategic memos. The technical name is a reasoning layer — Recognition-Rigor Framework, scored as a Decision Quality Index."*

The secondary one-liner for investor and regulatory-tailwind moments: *"The reasoning layer the Fortune 500 needs before regulators start asking."* — anchored in EU AI Act Art 14 (high-risk decision-support enforcement Aug 2026), SEC AI disclosure rulemaking, Basel III ICAAP, GDPR Art 22.

Banned vocabulary (never on cold or marketing surfaces): *"decision intelligence platform"* (Gartner-crowded, ceded to Cloverpop / Aera / Quantellia / Peak.ai), *"decision hygiene"* (Kahneman's term — using it cedes our category vocabulary to him), *"boardroom strategic decision"* (excludes funds + non-board-reporting CSOs), *"company knowledge base"* (dilutes the decision-specific moat into Notion/Confluence/Drive territory), any named prospect on public surfaces (Sankore stays inside Founder Hub).

## 4. Outcome Gate enforced on Individual tier (locked v3.2 — supersedes v3.1 pulse survey)

The single most consequential v3.2 fix. The v3.1 plan softened to a "did this audit change your decision?" pulse survey for Individual-tier subscribers — a friction-reduction move that NotebookLM correctly flagged as walking directly into Cloverpop's Dormant Data Flywheel trap. The $30M+ exit valuation depends on the Bias Genome data substrate, which requires CLOSED OUTCOMES (Brier-scored). A database of pulse-survey clicks creates a £300K/yr lifestyle utility, not the defensible enterprise data asset required for a $50–150M strategic acquisition.

**v3.2 lock:** keep Outcome Gate enforcement ON for Individual tier from day one. Engineer the friction OUT through automation and UX, not by lowering the bar.

**Friction-reduction mechanics (each shipped or near-shipped in codebase):**
1. **Auto-detection via Drive / Slack / email integrations** — `outcome-inference.ts` already infers closed outcomes from connected sources; wire for Individual tier specifically.
2. **One-click confirm-or-override draft outcomes** — Phase 3 of Outcome Gate already shipped (`OutcomeGateModal` pre-fills from auto-detected drafts; PATCH `/api/outcomes/draft action=confirm` resolves in one click).
3. **Outcome-gated feature unlocks** — Decision DNA, Bias Genome contribution panel, cross-org cohort comparison gate behind ≥3 closed outcomes. Framed as *"unlock by closing outcomes"* — outcome logging becomes the unlock mechanic, not the tax.

**The "why this matters" UX layer (new design spec, locked v3.2).** Every Outcome Gate moment surfaces an inline rationale — one sentence visible by default, expandable on click — that explains the value the user receives:

- **For closed outcomes:** *"Your closed outcome teaches the platform YOUR decision pattern. After 3 outcomes, your DQI calibrates to you — not the generic 143-case library. This is how Decision Intel gets sharper for the way you specifically decide."*
- **For pre-mortems:** *"Per Klein & Mitchell 1995, prospective hindsight produces 25–30% more failure-cause insights than 'what could go wrong?' framing. You're literally training your future-self to surface the failure modes you'd otherwise miss."*
- **For dispute / lessons-learned fields:** *"This becomes part of your Decision Knowledge Graph — the audit-committee artefact your CFO can pull up in 60 seconds, the institutional memory that survives team transitions."*
- **For cohort contribution stat (unlocks at ≥1 closed outcome):** *"You've sharpened {N} bias patterns across the cross-org cohort. Your audits are now part of the platform-wide calibration baseline."*

The discipline: every "why this matters" line is one sentence + cite-grade reasoning (the academic anchor — Klein & Mitchell, Kahneman, the 143-case calibration — does the persuasion). Not marketing copy. Not exclamation points. Procurement-grade voice. The user reads ONE sentence and understands what they're contributing to. The expand-on-click view goes deeper for the curious. The subtext: this is not boring data entry — this is a CSO training their decision instincts on their own historical pattern.

**The combined effect** of the friction-reduction mechanics + the why-this-matters layer + outcome-gated unlocks is that Outcome Gate becomes the **lifestyle of the platform** — not a tax users avoid but a unlock mechanism that compounds the platform's value to them specifically, and compounds the data moat that makes a $50–150M strategic acquisition defensible.

Positioning copy edit: *"Decision Intel audits the reasoning — whether a human or an agent produced it. Same biases, same regulatory exposure, same procurement-grade DPR."* No rebuild required; the 12-node pipeline accepts any structured artefact.

**Output of the investigation:** a one-page memo by end of June 2026 documenting the prospect-level evidence and recommending whether the audit-layer-for-agents pivot lands as the primary framing or stays as a Q3 contingency. Either way: the founder finds out NOW, not in October.

## 7. Goldner discovery questions (reframed for new wedge + ceiling)

**3 rules (literal):** talk to 10 people; find the pattern in their answers, not your assumption; sell to the pattern, not to the product you wish you'd built.

**4 questions — Individual CSO / M&A Head version (the wedge):**
1. *"Walk me through the last strategic decision you wrote up. Where did the writing happen — Google Doc, Slack threads, Confluence, board deck? And how much of the original reasoning made it through to the final artefact?"*
2. *"What's the question your CEO / board / parent company asked that you didn't see coming?"*
3. *"If you could replay one decision from the last 12 months knowing what you know now — what was the bias hiding in plain sight?"*
4. *"What's the artefact you wish you'd had to defend that decision when it was reviewed?"*

**4 questions — Corp Dev / Corp Strategy M&A version (the ceiling):**
1. *"Walk me through your last cross-border acquisition memo. Where did the diligence + assumption-setting happen, and how did you carry the structural assumptions (sovereign cycle, FX regime, regulatory exposure) into the IC deck?"*
2. *"What's the regulatory question — FCA, SEC, EU AI Act, GDPR, sovereign-context regime — that surfaced AFTER IC approval, when you wished it had surfaced before?"*
3. *"If you could replay one M&A approval from the last 24 months — what was the bias the room missed that the IRR / outcome later exposed?"*
4. *"What's the artefact your audit committee or GC would need to see to approve another deal of similar profile in the next 12 months — and what would it have to contain that today's diligence pack doesn't?"*
