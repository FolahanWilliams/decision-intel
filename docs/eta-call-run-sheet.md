# ETA Call Run Sheet

Founder-private prep for the booked meetings with searchers / independent sponsors / holdco operators. Read it before the call; glance at it during. Phone-readable by design.

> The whole motion in one line: **get a real deal in front of you, run the audit live, let ONE real finding land. The artifact persuades — you facilitate.** A signed yes follows a landed finding, never a pitch.

---

## The ONE goal of the call

NOT "close the sale." The goal is: **they put a real deal in front of you → you run the audit live → one genuine finding lands.** Everything else (sign-up, payment) follows from that moment. If you walk out having run the audit on their real deal and surfaced something they'd want caught before signing, the call worked — even if they don't sign on the spot.

---

## Before the call (2 minutes)

- **Have open in tabs:** `decision-intel.com/use/owner-operator-deal-audit` (the page that's literally for them) + `/demo` (ready to paste) + their LinkedIn / firm site (find ONE specific detail to mention).
- **Decide your ask in advance:** "set you up to run it on your next live deal this week." Know it before you walk in.
- **No slide deck. No deck.** The audit is the demo.

---

## The open — first 60 seconds (warm, not pitchy)

> "Thanks for the time. I'm not going to pitch you — honestly I'd rather just show you the thing working on a real deal than talk about it. Did you bring one you're looking at, or even one you've already closed?"

Disarms the "here comes a sales pitch" guard immediately. The **closed deal is the safe first share** — forensic, no risk to them, and the value still detonates.

---

## Get the deal (the pivotal move)

- **They brought one** → great, paste it.
- **They didn't** → fallback, in order:
  1. "No problem — give me a deal you know cold. Even a famous one, or one you passed on. I'll run it so you see the shape."
  2. "Or send me one right after this and I'll have it audited before we next talk." (Books the second touch.)
- **Never run on nothing.** A live audit on a real memo is the entire point.
- **Privacy worry** → "It's encrypted, the anonymizer strips names and prices as the first step, and nothing feeds anything cross-org. Redact the counterparty if you want — the reasoning still audits."

---

## Run it live (the wow) — ~60 seconds, then shut up

- Paste → let it run → when a real finding surfaces (the DSCR stretch, the comp they anchored on, the synergy they fell in love with), **STOP talking and let it land.** Do not narrate over it.
- The line, once it lands:
  > "That's the thing — it's not that your read is wrong. It's that in your setup there's no one to make you defend that one assumption before you sign. That's the second set of eyes."
- **NEVER:** "you're biased / your judgment is off."
- **ALWAYS:** name the missing process (no IC to force the defense), never broken judgment.

---

## The "it does this on every deal" beat → the site

> "This runs on every deal you look at — it builds your own underwriting trail, deal after deal. Here's the page." *(screen-share `/use/owner-operator-deal-audit`)*

- Self-value framing: **"the calibration record only you can build."**
- **DO NOT** say "a record your backers trust" or "hand this to a family office as proof." You haven't earned that yet — claiming it triggers "who are you?" Earn it through the value first.

---

## The ask (the close) — a next step, not "buy now"

> "Want me to set you up to run it on your next live deal this week? Takes a minute."

- The ask = sign-up + their next real memo.
- If they hesitate → "Run one yourself this week, then let's talk. If it catches something real on your own deal, you'll know it's worth it — and if it doesn't, we don't talk again."
- **If a finding landed → ask for the referral right then** (this is the aha moment): "Who else in your network is underwriting a deal right now?"

---

## The 3 objections (ego-safe, 1-line counters)

**"I know my own biases — my judgment is my edge."**
> "Your judgment IS the edge — this doesn't replace it. It gives it the one thing a solo buyer doesn't have: a second set of eyes on the single assumption that costs a deal. A fund partner has an IC to play that role. You don't. This is the risk desk, not a verdict on your thinking."

**"Isn't this just ChatGPT with extra steps?"**
> "The wrapper's the easy part — anyone can prompt a model. What you can't fake is a record of how every deal you underwrote actually held up, deal after deal. That accumulating track record is the part that's yours, and it's the part a tool can't recreate."

**"I don't have budget / I'm pre-close."**
> "Then run the free one on a live deal this week — no card. If it doesn't catch something you'd want caught before you sign a personal guarantee, we don't talk again. That's the whole offer."

---

## The disciplines (do not break these)

1. **The audit persuades. You facilitate. Talk less.** The silence after a finding lands is the sale.
2. **Self-value, never third-party-trust.** "Catch your own blind spot," not "a record backers trust."
3. **Ego-safe always.** Name the missing process (no IC), never broken judgment.
4. **Lead forward** (a live deal); the **retro** (a closed deal) is the safe fallback, not the lead.

---

## After the call (within the hour)

- **4-line follow-up:** thanks + the one finding that landed + the link + the one next step. Nothing more.
- **If it went well:** the referral ask — right after a real finding is the aha moment, so don't wait for a "thank you" email to ask.
