# Cold Email — "Co-author the next specimen"

**Strategic context:** Under-leveraged move #3 from the 2026-04-26 NotebookLM strategic synthesis. Each new sample DPR you build with a sceptical prospect's nominated case unlocks the next 50 prospects in their sector. Each specimen is a scalable GTM asset — the artefact does the persuasion, not your sentences (per `es_10`).

**When to send this:** to a sceptical buyer in a sector NOT yet covered by the WeWork (US public-market) or Dangote (Pan-African industrial) specimens. Examples of high-priority sectors to open: European growth equity, healthcare M&A, Middle East sovereign wealth allocation, Asian PE, US large-cap CSO, GCC Islamic-finance compliance.

**Empathic-mode rule applied:** the email starts from the recipient's POV. They get 50+ cold pitches a week. The opener earns 10 seconds; the ask earns 30. The artefact-led discipline (`es_10`) says give them an evidence anchor immediately.

## Operational notes

**Track the asks:** every cold email sent should be logged in the Founder Hub Outreach Pipeline with `purpose: "third-specimen co-author"`. Track response rate by template version and by recipient seniority.

**Conversion expectation:** ~30-40% reply rate (the ask is unusual + low-friction; people enjoy nominating cases). ~10% follow through with a nominated case. Each followed-through case = 1 new specimen DPR = the moat extends one sector.

**What to do with the nominated case:**
1. Verify it's publicly known (no NDA risk; the recipient suggested it precisely because it's public)
2. Pull the publicly-available pre-decision artefacts (S-1, board memo, IPO prospectus, public IC summary)
3. Run the audit pipeline end-to-end
4. Generate the DPR
5. Send back to the recipient with a single-paragraph note: "Here's the audit on [DEAL]. Three patterns surfaced — [bias 1], [bias 2], [bias 3]. Counterfactual lift if all three were mitigated pre-decision: [N] DQI points, ceiling [grade]. Worth a 20-min walk-through?"
6. Add the audit to `public/dpr-sample-[name].pdf` and to the case library
7. Reference in the next [SECTOR] cold email cycle

**Empathic discipline reminder:** the email is NOT a pitch. It's a curiosity ask wrapped in evidence. The moment the recipient reads "no commitment — I just want one sample your sector recognises," the empathic frame lands and the response rate jumps. Don't pitch in the body. Don't list features. The DPR itself does the persuasion in the follow-up.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- ❌ "We're a Y Combinator startup building AI for strategic decisions" — startup-coded, generic, ignores the empathic frame
- ❌ "Our 12-node pipeline detects 20 cognitive biases across 7 regulatory frameworks" — feature-led, jargon-leaking, banned per CLAUDE.md cold-context rules
- ❌ "Can I get 15 minutes on your calendar to learn about [FIRM]?" — discovery-call ask without offering anything; converts at <5%
- ❌ "Decision Intel is the native reasoning layer for every high-stakes call" — locked vocabulary in a cold context; the recipient doesn't know what a "reasoning layer" is yet (per `bd_8`)
- ❌ Naming any actual prospect in the email body (e.g., "Sankore is a design partner" — banned per CLAUDE.md no-named-prospects rule)
