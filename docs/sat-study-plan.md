# SAT Study Plan — companion to the SAT Prep tab

Founder-private. The `SAT Prep` founder-hub tab is the *engine* (daily drills, error
log, calibration loop, vocab SM-2, test log). This doc is the *reference* — the
backwards-planned schedule the tab encodes. When the two disagree, the tab's live
error log wins: the plan is weak-area-driven, so the *content* of each phase comes
from your data, not a fixed syllabus.

## The numbers

- **Baseline:** 1280 PSAT.
- **Target:** 1550+ (≈ +270 points, ~85th → ~99th percentile). Real, not a tune-up — at 1550 you can miss only a handful of questions total.
- **Budget:** 30 min/day. Enough *only* because the constraint forces ruthless targeting — every minute on weak skills + error review, zero generic practice.
- **Dates:** September benchmark (live conditions) → **November is the score that counts.** Stanford superscores, so the best section across dates composites.

## The three facts that change the calculus

1. **UC Berkeley is test-blind** — it will never see your SAT. This whole effort is for **Stanford + other test-requiring privates**. (Verify Stanford's live testing policy closer to your Nov-2027 application — it reinstated a requirement for recent cycles and is near-certain to require it by then.)
2. **The digital SAT is adaptive** (Bluebook). Question difficulty matters, not just count correct — only official Bluebook/Khan scoring is the truth. AI-generated drills are off-distribution; they're for reps on a diagnosed weak skill, never the score.
3. **Vocab is de-emphasised** — it's words-in-context reasoning now, not obscure-word recall. Keep the vocab block thin.

## The daily 30 minutes

1. **~3 min — review.** Open Progress → glance at your weakest skills + any overconfident-and-wrong misses.
2. **~22 min — targeted drilling.** Daily Training → pick your weakest skill (it pre-selects from the error log) → `Generate 3 drills`. **Tag your confidence *before* you answer** (this is the calibration loop). On a miss, tag the root cause.
3. **~5 min — vocab.** Vocab Bank → clear your due cards (SM-2).
4. **Mark the session complete.** The streak rewards *showing up*, never the score.

Once or twice a week, swap the drill block for a **timed Bluebook section** and log it. Re-take a full-length under timed conditions every ~10 days in the push phase.

## The phases (backwards-planned from November)

**Week 1 — Diagnostic.** One official Bluebook full-length, timed, real conditions. Log every miss (root cause + confidence). Produces the real R&W↔Math split + your top-5 weak skills. → *Exit: a baseline score + a populated error log.*

**Weeks 2–6 — Lock Math toward the ceiling.** Math is finite, pattern-based, and your edge (Calc, finance, music-theory distinction). Drive it to 780–800 first. Daily drills on weakest Math skills, interleaved; every miss → flashcard. Re-test a Bluebook Math section every ~2 weeks. → *Exit: Math sections landing 770+.*

**Weeks 6–12 — Grind R&W + September benchmark.** Shift weight to R&W (the harder grind): Words in Context, Command of Evidence, Transitions, Boundaries. Thin Math maintenance thread. **Sit the September SAT** — the rehearsal that exposes timing + nerves you can't fake at home. → *Exit: a real September score + isolated R&W weak spots.*

**Weeks 12–20 — Calibration push → November.** Hunt your **overconfident-and-wrong** questions (confidence 2–3 but incorrect = a baited System-1 pattern, the highest ROI on the test). Full-lengths every ~10 days, timed. Tighten pacing. **November is the score that counts.** → *Exit: a November sitting at the target band.*

## Why this beats a lesson teacher

Teachers teach to a median student with a slow feedback loop. This system is personalised to *your* error patterns, gives instant root-cause feedback, and applies the one thing you already think in — calibration. The overconfident-miss hunt is your bias thesis turned on yourself: find where your intuition is confident and wrong, and drill exactly there.
