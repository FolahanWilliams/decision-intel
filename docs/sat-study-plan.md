# SAT Study Plan — companion to the SAT Prep tab

Founder-private. The `SAT Prep` founder-hub tab is the _engine_ (daily drills, error
log, calibration loop, vocab SM-2, test log). This doc is the _reference_ — the
backwards-planned schedule the tab encodes. When the two disagree, the tab's live
error log wins: the plan is weak-area-driven, so the _content_ of each phase comes
from your data, not a fixed syllabus.

## The numbers

- **Baseline:** 1280 PSAT (Oct 2025) — **Reading & Writing 660 / Math 620.** Math is the weaker half. The per-domain "Knowledge and Skills" bands name the real targets: **Advanced Math is far the weakest (470–540 band, 32.5% of the Math section)**, then Algebra (610–670, 35%). In R&W only Craft & Structure is near-ceiling (680–760); Information & Ideas, Standard English Conventions, and Expression of Ideas all sit at 610–670.
- **Target:** 1550+ (≈ +270 points, ~85th → ~99th percentile). Real, not a tune-up — at 1550 you can miss only a handful of questions total.
- **Budget:** 30 min/day. Enough _only_ because the constraint forces ruthless targeting — every minute on weak skills + error review, zero generic practice.
- **Dates:** September benchmark (live conditions) → **November is the score that counts.** Stanford superscores, so the best section across dates composites.

## The three facts that change the calculus

1. **UC Berkeley is test-blind** — it will never see your SAT. This whole effort is for **Stanford + other test-requiring privates**. (Verify Stanford's live testing policy closer to your Nov-2027 application — it reinstated a requirement for recent cycles and is near-certain to require it by then.)
2. **The digital SAT is adaptive** (Bluebook). Question difficulty matters, not just count correct — only official Bluebook/Khan scoring is the truth. AI-generated drills are off-distribution; they're for reps on a diagnosed weak skill, never the score.
3. **Vocab is de-emphasised** — it's words-in-context reasoning now, not obscure-word recall. Keep the vocab block thin.

## The daily 30 minutes

1. **~5 min — Review tab.** Clear your due cards: each is a past miss — recall the concept + the trap _before_ you reveal it (retrieval beats re-reading), then self-grade. Hit "Explain this miss" on anything fuzzy. The tab badge shows how many are due.
2. **~20 min — targeted drilling.** Daily Training → pick your weakest skill (it pre-selects from the error log) → `Generate 3 drills`. **Tag your confidence _before_ you answer** (the calibration loop). On a miss, tag the root cause — it becomes a Review card automatically.
3. **~5 min — vocab.** Vocab Bank → clear your due cards (SM-2).
4. **Mark the session complete.** The streak rewards _showing up_, never the score — and it feeds your campaign XP on Start Here.

After a Bluebook full-length: **Test Log → "Log this test's misses"** — tap each skill you missed so your weak-area map + calibration are built on real questions (and each becomes a Review card).

Once or twice a week, swap the drill block for a **timed Bluebook section** and log it. Re-take a full-length under timed conditions every ~10 days in the push phase.

## The phases (backwards-planned from November)

**Week 1 — Diagnostic.** One official Bluebook full-length, timed, real conditions. Log every miss (root cause + confidence). Produces the real R&W↔Math split + your top-5 weak skills. → _Exit: a baseline score + a populated error log._

**Weeks 2–6 — Lock Math toward the ceiling.** Math is finite, pattern-based, and your biggest point reservoir — it's your _weaker_ half (620 vs 660 R&W), so the fastest points live here. Lead with **Advanced Math** (the 470–540 standout — content gaps are the most fixable error type), then **Algebra** (610–670, the biggest-weight Math domain at 35%). Leave Problem-Solving & Data Analysis and Geometry & Trigonometry on maintenance only — they're already near-ceiling (680–760). Daily drills on weakest Math skills, interleaved; every miss → flashcard. Re-test a Bluebook Math section every ~2 weeks. → _Exit: Math sections landing 770+._

**Weeks 6–12 — Grind R&W + September benchmark.** Shift weight to R&W's three mid-band (610–670) domains: Information & Ideas (Command of Evidence, Inferences, Central Ideas), Expression of Ideas (Transitions, Rhetorical Synthesis), and Standard English Conventions (Boundaries, grammar). Leave Craft & Structure on maintenance — it's already your strongest R&W domain (680–760). Thin Math maintenance thread. **Sit the September SAT** — the rehearsal that exposes timing + nerves you can't fake at home. → _Exit: a real September score + isolated R&W weak spots._

**Weeks 12–20 — Calibration push → November.** Hunt your **overconfident-and-wrong** questions (confidence 2–3 but incorrect = a baited System-1 pattern, the highest ROI on the test). Full-lengths every ~10 days, timed. Tighten pacing. **November is the score that counts.** → _Exit: a November sitting at the target band._

## Why this beats a lesson teacher

Teachers teach to a median student with a slow feedback loop. This system is personalised to _your_ error patterns, gives instant root-cause feedback, and applies the one thing you already think in — calibration. The overconfident-miss hunt is your bias thesis turned on yourself: find where your intuition is confident and wrong, and drill exactly there.
