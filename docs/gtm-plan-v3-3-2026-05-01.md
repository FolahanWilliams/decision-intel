## 7. Discovery + tailored-pitch hybrid motion (NEW v3.3)

The single most-load-bearing operational section of v3.3. Replaces v3.2 §7 (Goldner discovery questions) with the hybrid motion the founder explicitly chose given burn-rate constraints — discovery happens FIRST within each conversation (data integrity), THEN a pitch tailored to what they revealed (conversion capture).

**Why hybrid not pure Mom Test.** Pure 30-conversation discovery discipline (Rob Fitzpatrick, *The Mom Test*) maximises data quality but leaves runway burning while signal accumulates. The founder's burn-rate constraint forces the trade. The hybrid preserves data quality (discovery happens first within each conversation, before any pitch) while capturing conversion (after discovery, the pitch is genuinely tailored to what they revealed — not generic). Cost: ~10% data-quality erosion on later conversations as early-conversation pitches anchor founder framing. Benefit: revenue starts compounding while discovery accumulates.

**The 4 questions — discovery FIRST, no pitch yet.** Verbatim. Read the discovery card.

| # | Question | Why this question |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | *"Walk me through the last strategic memo, IC deck, or board paper you put together. What was the part you dreaded?"* | Forces specific instance, not general patterns. Surfaces real pain in their language, not yours. No leading toward your moat. |
| 2 | *"When the CEO, partners, or board pushed back, what was the one question that surprised you?"* | The surprise question IS the bias-they-missed signal — but they describe it as "the question I didn't see coming," not as "bias." Their language. |
| 3 | *"What's the last thing you bought or paid for to make that part less painful — and did it actually work?"* | Past-behavior question. Forces real spending evidence. If they've never bought anything to fix it, the pain isn't fundable yet — that's THE signal. |
| 4 | *"Who else is researching this space? Who should I talk to next?"* | Every discovery should produce 2 more conversations. Standard Steve Blank customer-development close. |

**The deflection script** (used if asked "what do you do?" before the founder has finished discovery): *"I'm researching whether a problem in strategic decision-making is real before I show anyone what I've built. Could I ask you 15 minutes about your work?"* Don't extend. Don't elaborate. Don't mention DPR / DQI / R²F / "reasoning layer" / "60-second audit" / the URL.

**The pivot to pitch — only AFTER all four discovery questions land.** The hybrid is: once the buyer has revealed pain, payment patterns, and the surprise-question signal, pivot with: *"Based on what you said about [their specific pain in their words], I think I have something you should see."* Then the tailored pitch.

**Tailored-pitch playbook — what to say keyed off what they revealed:**

| If they revealed (in Q1-Q3) | Pitch this | Avoid this |
|---|---|---|
| "The reasoning trail dies in Slack threads / Google Docs / four-tool graveyard" | *"Decision Intel is the system of record for that reasoning trail — every flag carries an excerpt + a regulatory citation; decision history survives team transitions; audit-committee Q&A pulls reasoning in 60 seconds."* | Don't lead with R²F or the 12-node pipeline — they revealed an ARTEFACT pain, pitch the artefact answer. |
| "We almost missed [X bias / blind spot] in the last memo" | *"The platform runs three professional lenses on every memo — equity-research skeptical, regulator-hostile, contrarian-strategist — and surfaces the bias each lens flags. Your memo would have caught [their blind spot] before the room did."* | Don't lead with the bias taxonomy count — they revealed a SPECIFIC failure mode, pitch the lens answer. |
| "We can't get the reasoning back when audit committee asks 6 months later" | *"The DPR is the artefact for exactly that moment — hashed and tamper-evident, EU AI Act Article 14 aligned, regulator-grade. Your audit committee gets the full reasoning trail in one PDF."* | Don't lead with regulatory tailwinds globally — they revealed a SPECIFIC audit-committee Q&A pain, pitch the DPR artefact answer. |
| "We're staring down a cross-border deal and the regulatory regime is a mess" | *"That's the cross-border M&A differentiator. The platform maps [their regions] flag-by-flag — NDPR if Nigeria, PoPIA if South Africa, WAEMU if Côte d'Ivoire — alongside EU AI Act + Basel III. Pitch the free audit: *"There's a free audit anyone can run on a memo at decision-intel.com — no login, 60 seconds. Want me to walk you through one on the memo you just described, no commitment?"* | Don't ask for paid pilot. The pain isn't fundable yet — get them on the free tier and let usage convert. |

Want to see it run on the memo you just described?"* | Don't deny the alternative is good — they're already a believer; convert by showing speed + provenance + price. |

**Persona-specific opener variants** (the discovery questions are the same; the OPENER framing varies by who they are):

- **CSO at FTSE 250 / S&P 500:** *"I'm researching how strategic memos get reviewed before the room sees them. Could I ask you about the last one you put together?"*
